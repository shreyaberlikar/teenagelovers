Chapter 5: Under the Stars, Just Us

🗓️ 01/01/2025
It was the first day of the new year — and Aavni was already teasing him over call.
“You think you know everything, huh?” she said with a flirty smirk in her voice.
“Meet me and I’ll show you what everything really means.”
Darshan chuckled. “When?”
“Now.”
Her voice was challenging, half-sweet, half-sassy — just the way he loved it.

By 6 PM, the winter sky had faded into a deep navy. The cold settled in, but so did anticipation.
At 7, he was outside her gate — car headlights cutting through the fog.
She slid into the passenger seat with a soft “Hi, bb,” and leaned over to kiss his cheek.
“Wanna know my new year resolution?” she asked.
“What?”
“To ruin your mind. And then fix it with cuddles.”

As they drove out of town, soft music played — their playlist.
Darshan’s hand kept brushing hers as he shifted gears, and each time he did, she smiled like it was the first time.
The silence was warm. Intimate. Like everything was understood without being said.
They reached their spot — tucked far from people and noise, surrounded by stars, trees, and secrets.

Outside, they stood for a moment — cold air on their skin, eyes on the sky.
Inside, they knew what was about to happen.

Back in the car — doors shut, world forgotten — time stopped existing.
Darshan looked at her and whispered,
“You look like a dream tonight, sunshine.”
She leaned closer, her voice soft against his lips,
“Then touch me like I’m not real…”
And he did.

She climbed onto his lap slowly, wrapping her arms around his neck. Their lips met — deep, desperate, dizzying.
His hands slid under her hoodie, finding the bare warmth of her waist. Her gasp hit the air like music.
His name escaped her lips like a secret prayer: “Darshan…”

Her hoodie slipped off her shoulders. His lips trailed her neck, collarbone, the slope of her chest — leaving warm, wet kisses with each breath.
“You’re so soft, cutie,” he murmured, “So perfect.”

Her hands gripped his hair, tugging, needing more. He leaned back to look at her — cheeks flushed, pupils wide.
“Tell me what you want,” he asked, breath heavy.
“You,” she whispered. “Every version of you. Every inch. Every sound.”

Their bodies pressed closer, heat building as clothes disappeared — tossed in the backseat like petals of a flower too eager to bloom.
He kissed her chest, her stomach, her inner thighs — slow, reverent, addictively tender.
She arched under his touch, hands all over him — marking his back with her nails, breathing his name like poetry:
“Darshan...… don’t stop.”

He moved inside her slowly, carefully — watching her expression, memorizing her every sound.
She clung to him, moaning into his shoulder, whispering:
“I love you in ways I can’t explain, and in places you can’t see.”
Their pace built. The windows fogged. The car moved gently with every rhythm.
Skin to skin… Soul to soul.
“Don’t let this night end,” she whispered.
“I won’t,” he replied, kissing her deeply, “Not until you’re mine in every way.”

And when they reached the edge — the moment where time collapsed and hearts exploded — they said nothing. Just breath. Just love. Just them.

Later, she lay against him, sweaty, breathless, glowing.
He brushed her hair off her forehead and smiled,
“Happy New Year, bb. You ruined me.”
She laughed, fingers tracing his jaw.
“Good. Now you’re mine forever.”

On the ride back, she recorded a mini vlog — lips swollen, cheeks pink.
The city lights came closer, but she didn’t want to leave the magic behind.
“You know,” he said quietly, “you once said you'd leave me in 2024…”
She turned to him, eyes full of something warm and unshakable.
“Promise me something?”
“What, cutie?” he asked, squeezing her hand.
“Promise you’ll never let me go.”
He looked at her with that intense gaze — the one that undressed everything but her clothes.
“I promise. But only if you stay mine too.”
“I always was.”

At the gate, before she stepped out, she kissed him again. Soft. Long. Final.
Then she whispered, “Thank you for making the first day of 2025 unforgettable, Darsh.”
He smiled. “Here’s to every day after, Aav.”
And the stars watched them fade into their own kind of forever. 💌💌💌
