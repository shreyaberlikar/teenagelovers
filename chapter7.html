Chapter 7: Confessions, Cadburys & The Chaos of Love

(aka when love started tasting like chocolate and touching like electricity)

From January 23, 2022 onwards:
“I loved you then. I love you still.”
Aavni didn’t blink. Didn’t breathe. The text just sat there on her screen like a mini heart attack.
And the worst part? She smiled.

“What if I feel it now?”
She couldn’t believe she sent that. Tani, her roomie and chaos partner, literally screamed from the bed next to her.
“YOU WHAT?! Girl, you just drop emotional nukes like that?”
“I dunno,” Aavni whispered. “It just… slipped.”

Darshan replied.
“Then I love you even more now.”
She swore her lungs stopped working. Screaming into pillows. K-drama vibes. Real love? Maybe.

February 12 – Chocolate Day Madness 🍫
Darshan messaged: “I’ll come on bike. Just five mins. I have something sweet for someone sweeter.”
She rolled her eyes, grabbed a Dairy Milk for him. At 5:37 PM sharp, he showed up—black shirt, Silk bar, rom-com vibes.

“For the girl who’s now officially mine.”
She giggled, gave him the Dairy Milk, and then—vroooom. Gone.
“Was that a date or a drive-thru?” she texted.
“Your face looked like dessert,” he replied.

March 9 – Shy Bridge, Shy Kids 🌉
First meet after confession. Nervous smiles. Hoodie hugs in spirit. They stood, laughed, teased. Nothing touched. But everything felt.
Their eyes said what their mouths didn’t.

March 10 – Birthday, Baby! 🎁
Her day. Her sparkle. Her magic.
He brought a small box: couple rings. Not gold. Not fancy. But them.
“For the girl who changed my year.”
They wore them. No kisses. Just warmth. Just love.

March 16 – First Hug & Tani’s Wild Tips
Tani’s dramatic advice on hugging and kissing was… chaotic.
“We don’t do awkward smudged lip kisses in this house!”
Aavni laughed. Panicked. Prepared?

Later that day…
He said, “C’mere.”
She slid into his arms. His hands on her back. Her heart: blender mode.
Her first hug — and it felt like flying.

March 22 – Kiss Me, Not There
He whispered, “Kiss me.”
She kissed his cheek.
“Not there.”
She panicked. Then… did it.
Slow. Soft. Real. Right.
Upper lip. Lower lip. Hoodie. Hands. Magic.
(PS: Tani’s hand-kiss advice? Worked. But don’t tell her.)

From Dairy Milks to couple rings, awkward silences to electric kisses —
they weren’t just falling.
They were diving.
Building their own universe of little firsts. 💗
