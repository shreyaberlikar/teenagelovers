Chapter 16: Wrapped in Blue, Unfolded in Silence

April 20th. Darshan’s birthday.

And Aavni was excited in a way only someone deeply in love can be.

She had spent the entire afternoon fussing over the perfect gift—a deep blue shirt with golden stripes, one she’d chosen with her best friend Teja.

They had laughed in the process, held it up against the sunlight, and imagined how it would look on him.

“It’s perfect,” she’d said, hugging it to her chest.

She didn’t stop there—his favorite chocolates, a folded note full of inside jokes, and a letter she never had the guts to say aloud.

She wasn’t expecting fireworks. Just a warm "Thank you."

⋆⋆⋆

They met the evening before his birthday.

He opened the gift casually. Aavni's heart thudded with anticipation.

But instead...

Disappointment 1: The Joke That Stung
He smirked, “Tuzhya aadhich khup janani wish kela re.”
A half-smile. A quiet crack inside her. She wasn’t asking to be first. Just the one who mattered most.

Disappointment 2: The Subtle Jab
“Thoda motha ahe na? I think it’s long for me.”
Her smile faded. She had double-checked everything. “Teja and I picked it for you,” she whispered. He shrugged.

Disappointment 3: The Heartpinch
“Mi ashe shirt nahi ghalat… But thanks re.”
He didn’t look at her. She clenched her fists behind her back.

She stood. “Kaay zhala?” he asked.
“Nothing. Just remembered something.”

⋆⋆⋆

That night, staring at the ceiling, she whispered to herself:

“You gave him love in a box—wrapped with thought, effort, and time.
He gave you half-hearted words and jokes that felt more like knives.”

For the first time in months, she didn’t feel like texting him goodnight.

“Sometimes, it’s not the big betrayals that break you.
It’s the little indifferences that quietly teach you what you mean to someone.”

💌 End of Chapter 16. Your heart deserves softness, not confusion.
