Chapter 25:
Darshan’s POV: Guilt Is Loud at 2 A.M.

“He made mistakes. Terrible ones. But his love wasn’t a lie—just deeply flawed, and too afraid to lose what it couldn’t replace.”

The streetlight buzzed softly outside. Darshan sat on Yash’s bedroom floor, phone blank beside him.
“I messed up, bro,” Darshan whispered.

“She wasn’t just any girl. Aavni… she was home.”
“She trusted me with everything. And what did I do? Lied. Took her love for granted.”

Yash leaned forward. “You love her, right?”
“More than anyone,” Darshan said. “But I… I broke her.”

“She hugged me after everything. Still hoping I’d be the guy she believed in.”
“I didn’t cheat with my heart. But I betrayed her trust.”
“I should’ve been honest from the start.”
“I don’t even know who I was trying to protect—her or myself.”

Yash sighed. “Then fix it, idiot. Earn her back. Be the guy she fell for.”
Darshan nodded. “I’ll do anything. I just hope… it’s not too late.”

The Second Chance

The night had swallowed her. Tears dried—but her heart still bled.
She should’ve walked away.
But she didn’t.

She remembered Teja. How even she gave a second chance.
Not because she was weak, but because love makes you brave.

That morning, hands trembling, she typed:
Aavni: Let’s try. But don’t make me regret it, Darshan.

His reply came fast:
Darshan: I promise. I swear—I won’t lose you again.
A part of her wanted to believe. Another warned her.
But she silenced doubt—for now.

No grand gestures. Just emotion-laced silence.
He held her hand gently.
“I’ll never lie again, Aavni.”
She nodded—but stayed silent. He had to earn it all back.

In the weeks that followed:
Long drives. Midnight calls. Laughter.
But the innocence was gone.

What remained was love tested by fire.
Burnt—but not destroyed.
And that made it stronger.

“She didn’t take him back because she forgot. She took him back because she remembered everything… and still hoped love could win.”
