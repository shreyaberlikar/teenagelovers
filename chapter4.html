Chapter 4: Backseat Stars & Bus Stop Sass

📅 16th December — and Aavni had just survived the last battle of her semester — the OOPCG practical. And oh, what a ride that was.

Originally, she'd been given CG practical — the kind of subject that could emotionally damage even toppers. But thanks to a little convincing (okay, more like lowkey begging), she got herself swapped into OOPCG.

“Baba, ekdum zero errors run kela me! Compiler pan emotional zhala asel!”

“You didn’t just convince sir… you emotionally manipulated the system.”

“Arrey when survival is at stake, you gotta use the ‘girl charm’ + ‘code tears’ combo!” Aavni smirked.

Once she exited the lab, grinning like she'd won a trophy, she dialed the usual suspects — Aai-Baba, and Darshan. “Guess who’s the next OOPCG queen?”

“Not me,” Darshan deadpanned. “Exactly,” she winked through the phone.

By 1 PM, she was back at the PG. Packing was chaos: Half-open zippers, overfed bags, one missing sock, and three emotional breakdowns later, she was done. Ate. Showered. Got ready. And waited.

“I swear, this bus has the same punctuality as my ex’s promises,”

“At least the bus shows up eventually.”

At 5:59 sharp, they were on board. Bags above, headphones in, tea bottle ready.

The journey home? ICONIC. They gossiped like school never ended —

  • “Remember that guy who thought he was Shah Rukh in 9th?”
  • “His notes had more love poems than actual answers.”
  • “Her villain arc started after PT teacher called her bhendi!”

“You’re a Pisces. Which means emotional AF.”

“Excuse me, I’m a Pisces sun and savage moon.”

📅 1 AM — her dad came to pick her up. Darshan had teased: “Should I come pick you up with roses and Shahrukh arms?”

“Naah, my baba > Bollywood hero.” But her cheeks were warm.

That evening: cool wind, dreamy sky, Darshan’s car.

“I need a long drive, you, and emotional support.”

“You’ll get two out of three.”

“Warning: I may cry, laugh, and confess a random crush from 8th grade tonight.”

“If it’s not me, I’m dropping you home.”

“You were always the crush. But that mushroom cut…”

Darshan: “That was a trend!”

“Not in this galaxy.”

They cuddled, whispered, kissed under stars. 🩷

“We need a podcast.”
“2 idiots in love – Season 1.”

“Pull over,” she teased.
“You gonna confess again?”
“Nope. Just wanted to see you panic.”

In between laughs, playlists, winter wind and glances — they fell in love a little harder. ❤️
