Chapter 9: After the Door Closed

Part 1: Aavni’s Thoughts — Alone, but Glowing…

The door clicked shut behind him. She stood there for a moment—barefoot, her hair slightly messed up, the bedsheet still warm from where he’d held her.

Silence. But not empty.

Her heart was still racing, but slower now. Softer. Happier. The air in her room smelled faintly of his perfume… and chai. Her favorite mix now.

She walked back to the mirror, touched her lips, smiled to herself, then hid her face in the pillow like a teenager who’d just kissed her crush for the first time.

“He didn’t just kiss me today. He knew exactly when to hold, when to pause, when to look into my eyes and ask without asking.”

Her body still hummed with his touch. And her mind? A loop of everything he had said, from “You're trembling” to “Your lips are sweeter than this tea.”

She changed into her favorite pajamas and flopped back on the bed, hugging the pillow he had used.

“Was this love?” she whispered. “Or something even deeper?”

Part 2: Darshan’s POV — While Riding Away…

He rode slowly through their lane, a lazy smile stuck to his lips. Helmet on, but blush visible.

Bro: Bola hota to party karaychi hoti. What happened? Darshan: [no reply]

He didn’t reply. Because words felt too small right now.

He remembered how her fingers tugged his t-shirt, how she gasped softly when he kissed her stomach. The way she giggled and said, “Bas kar, Darshu,” while pouring chai.

And when she kissed him goodbye at the door—just once, softly on the cheek—he knew that moment would live in his bones.

“She let me in,” he whispered. “And not just inside the gate.”
