Chapter 28:
Still Yours, Still Mine

“They weren’t the same people—but the love never forgot their names.”

It had been years—yet some things hadn’t changed.
Darshan still smiled like he held a thousand secrets behind his lashes, and Aavni still looked at him like he was both the trouble and the cure.

They weren’t the same teenagers anymore. Not entirely.
But somehow, sitting across from each other in that café, sipping chai with cold fingers intertwined, they felt like it.

“Do you remember our first ride to McD?” Aavni asked, grinning, her voice soft with nostalgia.
Darshan laughed. “You spilled Coke all over the seat and blamed me.”
“Because it was your fault!” she teased, flicking sugar at him.

They broke into laughter—free, effortless, the kind that only lives between people who’ve seen each other break and still stayed.

A Love That Didn’t Expire

They had changed. Grown. Cried. Fought. Nearly walked away.
But the love? It had only deepened.

Now, he didn’t just hold her hand during a movie. He held her when she cried after a tough exam.
She didn’t just wait for his calls. She called him out when he was wrong—and he listened.

“I still fall in love with you every time you tie your hair like that,” he whispered, brushing a strand from her cheek.
She looked away, biting her lip.
“And I still blush like an idiot when you say things like that,” she whispered back.
They sat in silence, eyes doing the talking.

“You know what scares me?” he asked softly.
She tilted her head.
“That someday, this might not be enough for you.”
She took his hand, laced her fingers through his.
“Don’t be stupid, Darsh. You were my chaos. Now, you’re my calm.”

That night, they sat on the hood of his car, under the sky that had seen every version of them—angry, wild, in love, broken, repaired.

The stars above blinked quietly as he kissed her temple, and she rested her head on his shoulder.

Their world had changed.
But their love? It hadn’t faded.
It had only learned to survive.
