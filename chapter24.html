Chapter 24:
The Breakdown Beneath the Streetlight

“Sometimes, it’s not the cheating that breaks you… it’s the fact that you believed in them so much, you never thought they could.”

Aavni’s hands trembled around her phone. Her vision was blurred, not from the cold January night—but from the storm raging inside her.
She had called him once. Twice. Three times. No answer.

“He’s sleeping,” she thought bitterly.
She stepped outside, one thought echoing: I need answers.
Each step felt heavier. Her breath shallow, chest tight. Tears pressed at her eyes—but she swallowed them.

She called again. This time, he picked up.
“Come outside.”
Darshan sounded half-asleep. “What happened…?”

He saw her from the gate. She thrust her phone at him.
“Read it.”

On the screen—messages from Anjali.
His expression crumbled. His phone slipped, crashing to the pavement.

“I trusted you!” she screamed. “You were my world, Darshan. And you were someone else’s too?”
She cried harder than ever. Her entire body shook.

Rohan rushed over, “Guys, please, not here. Darshan... say something!”
But Aavni couldn’t contain it—SLAP.
“Why did you do this to me?” she sobbed.
Rohan held her, “Aavni, listen. Let him explain once.”
She nodded faintly.

They met again. Just the two of them.
Darshan stood silently.
“Mazya madhye kay kami hota?” she asked. “What was I not enough in, Darshan?”

He stepped forward, eyes glistening. “You were enough. Always more than enough.”
“Then why?”
“She texted first. It happened... I swear, barely a month. I stopped replying.”

“She said she’d harm herself. Cut her veins. She blackmailed me.”
“You could’ve told me,” Aavni muttered. “You lied instead.”
“I didn’t want to lose you. I showed her our chats. Told her I had someone.”
“But I lost trust,” she said, “You broke it with your silence.”
“I love you,” he whispered. “Please, don’t leave me like this.”

They stood there, hearts bruised. She stepped forward—
Hugged him. Tight.

But even wrapped in his arms… she didn’t feel peace.
Only pain. Only confusion.
A love too scarred to feel whole again.
